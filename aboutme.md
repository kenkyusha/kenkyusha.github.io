Originally from [Estonia](https://e-estonia.com), growing up in a tiny village, where the long fields and the forest accompanied most of my childhood. Little did I know where the journey would lead me...

<!-- ![village](/img/village.jpg) -->
<div style="text-align:center"><img src="/img/village.jpg" /></div>

My journey with machine learning began during my studies at the [Friedrich-Alexander University](https://www.fau.eu) in Germany around the beginning of 2015. Back then it was still the early days and I was wondering which direction I should steer with my studies and then I stumbled upon the [GoogleNet](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1409.4842.pdf) publication, which sparked me the interest for everything related to machine learning, deep learning, neural networks and so on. 

From that point on I steered my electives at the university and my student work projects from ML prospective and got a student job at the [International Audio Laboratories Erlangen](https://www.audiolabs-erlangen.de), a department between the university and the [Fraunhofer Institute](https://www.iis.fraunhofer.de/en.html).

One of the first projects I worked on was an instrument [extraction with deep neural networks](http://150.162.46.34:8080/icassp2015/pdfs/0002135.pdf) using stacked auto encoders. I had my first experience using programming languages such as [Theano](http://deeplearning.net/software/theano/) and [Lasagne](https://lasagne.readthedocs.io/en/latest/). I also wrote my thesis (MSc) titled "Soft Decision Trees for Singing Voice Detection", where I was working on was a novel audio domain research question, namely the singing voice [classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statistical_classification) from an audio frame using [decision trees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decision_tree) and novel approach of soft-decision trees (a mix of decision trees and neural networks).

After finishing my degree at the end of 2016, I started working at the [Fraunhofer Institute](https://www.iis.fraunhofer.de/en.html) on position estimation problems. My work has also received international recognition, first the publication at the [Indoor Positioning and Indoor Navigation](https://ipin2018.ifsttar.fr/awards/best-paper/) where I got the best paper award. Followed by extended research publication at the [MDPI Sensors](https://www.mdpi.com/1424-8220/19/5/1064).
