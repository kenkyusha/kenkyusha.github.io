Over the years I've worked on different projects covering different machine learning fields. Obviously the field has exploded there have been every year so many new developments that it feels it's really hard to be specialist in all of them but rather specialize. 

First the amount of computation data available and labeled datasets gave birth to boom in supervised learning which till today has been one of the most used throughout industry and also in academics. However, for more complex problems more adaptable ways of learning have to be employed such as unsupervised learning and reinforcement learning. Reinforcement learning has many advantages over supervised learning. One of them being that we do not really need a labeled dataset in order to train a model. Unfortunately, desiging a good reward system and training a model is much more complicated. Every supervised learning problem can be turned into a reinforcment learning problem, and this is where mostly people start and later they would eventually go for reinforcement learning.
